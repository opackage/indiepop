Indie as "independent"

Indie pop (sometimes as “Indie-pop” or indiepop) is a music genre as well as a subculture. Indie is the shortened word for “independent”, as the music style originated from independent music production and distribution efforts.

Characteristics

As a music genre, indie pop shows a few important characteristics: 1) it inherits a DIY music approach from punk; 2) it appeared as guitar-driven at early stages; 3) it’s different from mainstream pop in instrumentation, production, lyrics, social and political meanings; 4) it’s more melodic and sounds lighter than indie rock.

Indie Pop Chronicle

Indie pop was the same as “Indie” in the beginning. Indie is the shortened expression of “independent”, which refers to some bands (e.g. Buzzcocks) and record labels (Factory Records) pioneered to make and distribute music independently in the British post-punk period. Following the similar independent spirit, some bands (e.g. Television Personalities, Orange Juice, Felt) and labels (e.g. Postcards Records) emerged to release music sounded more melodic and softer compared to traditional rock music. This new music scene marks the start of indie pop.

Indie pop evolved from a mere music production approach (e.g. amateurish, DIY, personal) to a music genre that displays certain shared aesthetical traits, including jangly guitar sound, pop music harmonies. NME’s C86 compilation marked the maturity of indie pop. During this time, indie pop also started to rise in the U.S. (e.g. Beat Happening).

Indie pop broke into mainstream starting from mid-1980s as bands like The Smiths appeared on commercial charts. Indie pop became “cool”, the very idea that the indie bands chose to be away several years ago. Indie pop was at its renaissance during this period as it inspired many music genres and successful bands, for example Grunge band Nirvana.

The success of Indie pop in the 90s drove itself to mainstream. There were bands were even regarded as “mainstream indie”. As a music genre and subculture ideology, Indie pop was losing its edge due to the commercialization and homogeneity in late 1990s. Thanks to the technology advance especially the internet, Indie pop revived and diversified not long after entering the new Millennium. With the wide availability of high-quality music equipment and accessible online social network, it’s seeing a flourish of indie pop bands/artists with diversified styles, and a balanced reception and authenticity. 

Bands

The influential indie pop bands in history

Orange Juice

An indie and jangle pop band from Scotland, active mainly from 1979-1985.

Beat Happening

An American indie pop band from Olympia, Washington. Active 1982-1992.

The Smiths

An indie band from Manchester, England. Active from 1982 to 1987.

Heavenly

An indie band from Oxford, England. Active from 1989 to 1996.

Subgenres

Indie pop evolved into different styles over the years and inspired several prominet subgenres.

Twee Pop

It is characterized by simplicity and perceived innocence, some of its defining features are boy-girl harmonies, catchy melodies, and lyrics about love.

Chamber Pop

Chamber pop or Baroque pop is a subgenre of indie pop “that features lush orchestrations” Arcade Fire is an good example of this subgenre.

Bedroom Pop

It emerged in the last 10 years. Musicians can make high quality music in bedrooms with inexpensive gears, hence it’s called “bedroom” pop.
